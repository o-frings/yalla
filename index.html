How the scores work

Hypertrophy is the set-weighted average of each exercise's growth rating (loaded stretch, full range, loadability). Balance weighs muscle coverage, how evenly volume is spread, and push/pull and quad/posterior-chain symmetry — lopsided programs and gaps lower it.

  • Schoenfeld BJ, Grgic J, et al. (2017). Dose-response relationship between weekly resistance training volume and muscle hypertrophy. Journal of Sports Sciences, 35(11).
  • Baz-Valle E, et al. (2022). A systematic review of the effects of training volume on muscle hypertrophy. PLoS ONE, 17(6).
  • Schoenfeld BJ, et al. (2016). Effects of resistance training frequency on hypertrophy: a meta-analysis. Sports Medicine, 46(11).
